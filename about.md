<h1 class="post-title">About</h1>
<div class="post-content">

<p>I'm Rohit Chandramouli, a recent Electrical and Electronics Engineering graduate based in Tamil Nadu, India. This site records a six-month foundation sprint into ML engineering &mdash; Python and mathematical foundations, PyTorch, transformer architecture, LLM fine-tuning, and a flagship project combining retrieval, agents, and game AI into a working NPC system.</p>

<p>I'm writing this in public for two reasons. First, public accountability is a better forcing function than a private notebook &mdash; if a derivation or a system is wrong, someone can tell me before I build the next five things on top of it. Second, I'd rather the record of how I actually got good at this be visible, including the parts that didn't go to plan, than present a polished version after the fact.</p>

<p>I came to ML from outside the standard CS pipeline, through control systems and signal processing rather than a curriculum where gradient descent is day-one material. I'm especially interested in reinforcement learning and game AI &mdash; agents that act on a world model instead of a script, NPCs with real memory &mdash; and I think games are a strange, underused proving ground for ML ideas precisely because they're fully observable and infinitely repeatable. Where that interest eventually goes is an open question I'm not trying to answer in advance of doing the work.</p>

<h2>What this blog is not</h2>
<p>Not a tutorial site, and not a highlight reel. The posts here include dead ends, wrong derivations caught late, and weeks that didn't go as planned, alongside the things that worked. That's the more useful record, for me and for anyone reading along.</p>
