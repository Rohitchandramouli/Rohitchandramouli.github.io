<!--
LINKEDIN WEEKLY POST TEMPLATE

Workflow: write the blog post first (_drafts/week-template.md), then come
here and adapt it. Don't write this cold — it should pull its honesty
directly from what you just wrote for the blog, compressed and reshaped
for a feed that rewards standalone, scannable thoughts over status reports.

Posting cadence: every week, same week as the blog post. Don't skip a
LinkedIn post because the week felt "too quiet" to share — that's the
same highlight-reel trap the blog template was built to avoid. A short,
honest "this week was mostly debugging and it was frustrating" post is
fine and still worth posting.

Format rules that actually matter for LinkedIn specifically:
- 3-5 short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph, not one sentence per line.
- Open with the single most interesting/honest thing from the week, not
  "Week N update" — nobody is hooked by a label.
- The blog link goes at the END, framed as "more depth here" — never as
  the opening or the point of the post. Posts that read as "here's a
  link" get suppressed by LinkedIn's own algorithm; posts that read as
  a complete thought, with a link as a bonus, don't.
- No hashtag stacking. Two or three relevant ones at the very end, if any.
- Don't summarize the whole week. Pick ONE thread — the hardest part, the
  thing that surprised you, the thing you're proud of — and follow it.
  The blog post can hold everything; this post holds one thing well.

-->
